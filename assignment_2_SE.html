Question :-Write a tools for requirement gathering in software?
Answer:-

Requirements gathering (also called requirement elicitation) is the process of collecting information from stakeholders to understand what the software system should do.
There are different tools and techniques used for requirements gathering:


    1. Interviews:-
  • Direct discussion with stakeholders, clients, or users.
  • Can be structured or unstructured.
  • Helps to understand detailed requirements.

    2. Questionnaires / Surveys:-
  • Written set of questions given to users.
  • Useful when many users are involved.
  • Saves time and cost.

    3. Observation:-
  • Analyst observes users while they perform tasks.
  • Helps to understand real system behavior.
  • Useful when users cannot clearly explain their needs.

    4. Workshops:-
  • Group meetings of stakeholders and developers.
  • Used for discussing and finalizing requirements.
  • Helps in faster decision making.

    5. Brainstorming:-
  • Group discussion to generate ideas.
  • Encourages creative thinking.
  • Used in early stage of requirement gathering.

    6. Prototyping:-
  • Creating a simple model of the system.
  • Users give feedback on prototype.
  • Helps to clarify unclear requirements.

    7. Use Case Diagrams:-
  • Describe interaction between user and system.
  • Helps to understand system functionality.

    8. Document Analysis:-
  • Studying existing system documents.
  • Helps to understand current process and problems.

    9. Joint Application Development (JAD):-
  • Structured meeting between stakeholders and developers.
  • Fast requirement collection method.

    10. Requirement Management Tools (Software Tools):-

Some popular tools:
  • JIRA – Requirement tracking and management
  • Trello – Task and requirement management
  • Microsoft Visio – Creating diagrams
  • IBM Rational DOORS – Advanced requirement management
  • Google Forms – Conducting surveys
